Consent to Participate in a Research Study


  • Research Project Title: Measuring and Supporting File Management on Google Drive
  • Principal Investigator: Blase Ur
  • Graduate Student: Will Brackenbury
  • Sponsor: National Science Foundation
  • IRB Protocol #: IRB20-1671


Purpose of Research Study

You are being asked to participate in a research study about users’ management and organizational strategies for files in their Google Drive accounts. The purpose of this research study is to determine whether recommendations can assist file management activities in personal cloud storage. Ultimately, this research may be published and presented at scientific conferences to improve the community’s knowledge about file management and organization in personal cloud storage. You are encouraged to ask any questions about the information in the consent form using the contact information below before consenting to the research.

Prolific workers who have a 95%+ approval rating, have more than 10 submissions, who are age 18+ and live in the United States are eligible. In addition, you must have a Google Drive account that was created at least 3 months ago. The account must contain 100 or more files.


Participation Procedures and Activities

In Part 1 of this study, you will answer some questions about your experience with personal cloud storage and provide demographic information. Additionally, in order to enable some functionality in Part 2 of the study, we ask you to log in to your Google Drive account and give permission for our application to perform an automated scan. We do not store file content: however, file names, folder names, objects recognized in images, and some words from documents are saved in order to study the links between types of files and management strategies. We also ask for permission to view your Google Drive activity history, and store data about the types of actions you took at what times, as well as what files you acted upon. We will never have access to your password, and we do not store your username in the course of our study. You can read more about how Google allows third parties to access your account, and how you can manage this access here. For images stored in your account, we may use software provided by the Google Vision API. It is the policy of the Google Vision API not to access or use data provided to them except for the purposes of performing offered services (Google Cloud Terms). We save information about the types of objects the software recognized (e.g., whether there appears to be a kite in the photo), but we do not save the files themselves.

Part 1 takes 15 minutes on average and you will be compensated $2.50 for its completion.

If you meet the eligibility criteria, you may be invited to Part 2 of the study. In Part 2, we ask you to organize your Google Drive account in a sandbox environment using our custom file management interface. Changes in the sandbox environment have no effect on your Google Drive account. We also ask you several questions about your experiences using the interface. Part 2 takes 1 hour on average and you will be compensated $10 for its completion.

Due to the difficulty of determining credit for partial completion, no compensation will be provided for partial completion. This applies to both Part 1 and Part 2. However, compensation is awarded separately—full completion of Part 1 will result in full compensation for that part, regardless of the partial or full completion status of Part 2.


Risks/Discomforts of Being in this Study

As with any online service, there is a chance that a malicious user can “take over” the service in question without your knowledge; although unlikely, a breach of our service would allow that user full access to the cloud storage accounts of anyone actively using our service. We follow security best practices, and you can limit or remove our service’s access to your account at any time as detailed above. Aside from this, the risks to your participation in this online study are those associated with basic computer tasks, including boredom, fatigue, mild stress, or breach of confidentiality.


Benefits of Being in this Study

The only benefit to you is the learning experience from participating in a research study. The benefit to society is the contribution to scientific knowledge.


Confidentiality of Data and Limits to Confidentiality

Your Prolific ID will be used to distribute payment to you. Please be aware that your Prolific ID can potentially be linked to information about you, depending on your profile settings. We will not be accessing any personally identifying information about you that you may have put on your Prolific profile page.

Any reports and presentations about the findings from this study will not include your name or any other information that could identify you. The data will be stored temporarily on a secure server at the University of Chicago, and then analyzed and archived indefinitely on the secure research and development server of the SUPERgroup research lab at the University of Chicago.


Use of Your Research Data

Your study data consists of:

  1. Answers to survey questions for both Part 1 and Part 2
  2. Metadata about files (e.g., file name, file size, file location within the folder hierarchy)
  3. Data computed from file content (e.g., keywords in file, objects recognized in images, names of data table headers, similarity of content between files)
  4. Record of previous activity in Google Drive
  5. Records of actions recorded during the organizational task in Part 2

These data components may be analyzed and published in scientific conference proceedings in aggregate form. The free-text responses you provide to any portion of the surveys in this study may be quoted in part or in whole in this publication. We will remove any information from this analysis that could identify you before providing the analysis for publication.

To improve research in file management, we further ask permission to anonymize your study data and include it in a reference dataset that may be shared with researchers at other institutions. You are NOT required to provide permission for your data to be included in this anonymized dataset in order to continue with the study. If you choose not to offer permission, there will be no repercussions for you, nor will your compensation for this study be affected in any way.


GDPR Rights Respecting Your Study Data

If you reside in the European Economic Area during your participation in the study, the GDPR gives you certain rights with respect to your study data. You have the right to request access to, rectification, or erasure of, your study data. You also have the right to object to or restrict our processing of your study data. Finally, you have a right to request that we move, copy, or transfer your study data to another organization. In order to make any such requests, please contact the persons or entities listed below.


Voluntary Participation and Right to Refuse or Withdraw

Participation in this study is voluntary. The decision to participate in this study is entirely up to you. You may refuse to take part in the study at any time without prejudice or penalties to you and will not result in any loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. If you withdraw your consent, this will not affect the lawfulness or our collecting, use, and sharing of your study data up to the point in time that you withdraw your consent. Even if you withdraw your consent, we may still use or maintain your study data that identifies you to comply with our legal and regulatory requirements.
